About the Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the five big cats in the genus Panthera and a member of the family Felidae. The term "African lion" generally refers to several subspecies found across Africa. Males can exceed 250 kg (550 lb), making lions the second-largest living cat after the tiger.

Lions currently exist in sub-Saharan Africa and in India’s Gir Forest National Park. Historically, they ranged across much of Africa, Europe, Asia, and even the Americas until about 10,000 years ago.

Today, lions are classified as a vulnerable species. Populations have declined by 30–50% over recent decades, mainly due to habitat loss and human conflict. Lions outside protected reserves and parks face the highest risk, with West African lions being especially endangered.
